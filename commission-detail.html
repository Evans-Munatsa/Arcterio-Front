Countryside Chic

Average Area: 200sqm
Year: 2004

Creating A Self-Sustaining Sanctuary

Our brief was simple; design a house to accommodate the family’s specific needs and to retain a large open garden for children to play. Allowing the topography to dictate the house’s level, it became a multiple level house with each level, becoming a well-defined function of the house.

Although the house is connected to the grid, the design employs a rainwater harvesting system and supplements grid power with solar power; making this a self-sustaining sanctuary.

The exterior features a harmonious blend of natural stone and modern glass, creating a striking yet elegant facade. The expansive use of glass doors invite an abundance of natural light, blurring the lines between indoors and outdoors.

Seamless Flow of Space & Light

The simplicity of the house reflects the casual country side living that inspired the design. The open-plan layout fosters a seamless flow between the kitchen, dining, and living area with its central fireplace, enhancing the sense of spaciousness.

Attention Was Given To Detail To Provide A Canvas To Be
Coloured In As A Family’s Story Unfolds.

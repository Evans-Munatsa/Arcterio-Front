How To Avoid Budget Shocks
With Your Home Build

Written by Author Name Surname

“Foresee potential challenges and incorporate solutions into the design phase, saving time and money in the long run.”

Consider the experience of a homeowner who initially opted for council drawings to get quotes for their dream home. They found the resulting quotes widely varied, and as construction began, the costs quickly escalated due to unforeseen details and interpretations. Frustrated, they turned to a professional architect who provided complete construction drawings. The new quotes were higher but more accurate, reflecting the true scope of the project. This clarity allowed the homeowner to make informed decisions, ultimately saving money by avoiding unexpected costs and ensuring the project stayed within budget.

When embarking on a construction project, be it your dream home or a commercial building, managing costs effectively is crucial. There is a common belief (Supposition) that involving professional architects adds unnecessary expense. However, there are plenty incomplete projects one can find that demonstrate how their expertise can save you money in the long run by providing clarity on project costs through precise construction documentation.

Comprehensive Planning

Many people hold the view that hiring professional architects is an unnecessary expense, thinking they can rely solely on contractors or basic drawings to get their projects done. However, this approach can lead to significant misunderstandings and unexpected costs.

Professional architects bring a wealth of expertise and creative vision to the table. They have the skills to turn your ideas into functional and aesthetically pleasing designs, tailored to your specific needs and preferences. Their training and experience ensure that they can foresee potential challenges and incorporate solutions into the design phase, saving time and money in the long run.

Council Drawings

Council drawings are typically the first step in the construction documentation process. These drawings are used to obtain initial quotations but often lack detailed information on building materials, interior fixtures, fittings, and finishes. This limited information can lead to wide interpretations and discrepancies in cost estimates.

Example:
Three contractors were approached with council drawings, resulting in quotes of R9,000/sqm and R8,500/sqm. The lack of detail led to significant variations in the estimates, leaving room for potential misunderstandings and unexpected costs during the construction phase.

Basic Construction Drawings

In the next stage, basic construction drawings are created, adding more detail to the council drawings. These focus on the shell of the building, specifying materials for the external facades but still leaving out detailed specifications for internal fittings and finishes.

Example:
With added information, three different contractors provided quotes ranging from R13,700/sqm to R10,500/sqm. While this stage offers better clarity than council drawings, it still falls short of providing a complete picture, leading to variability in cost estimates.

Complete Drawings

The most detailed level of documentation is the complete construction drawings. These include comprehensive schedules for windows, doors, electrical layouts, ceiling details, floor finishes, wall finishes, and even specifics like ironmongery, sanitary schedules, and detailed cabinetry designs.

Example:
With added information, three different contractors provided quotes ranging from R13,700/sqm to R10,500/sqm. While this stage offers better clarity than council drawings, it still falls short of providing a complete picture, leading to variability in cost estimates.
